How to Encourage Good Oral Health
Healthy teeth grow and strengthen as a result of healthy living, so teach your little one to make smart decisions from the start.

See the Dentist
Pediatric dentists recommend scheduling baby's first dental appointment within six months of the first tooth's arrival, and/or between your little one's first and second birthdays. During the visit, the dentist will gently clean the teeth and gums, looking for discoloration or injury, and, depending on the practice, will apply a fluoride treatment. To maintain good oral health, bi-annual visits are encouraged.

Say No to Sugar
As you introduce solid foods to your child, it's important to prevent tooth decay—the #1 chronic children's disease in the country—by limiting sugar, specifically refined sugars and sticky natural sugars, and watering down the no more than 4 to 6 ounces of juice that he or she may drink daily. Indulge your little one in a post-meal ISSA mikro tooth cleaning to allow the "silicone brush gently and effectively wash away milk and bacteria from teeth and gums, preventing early childhood caries," suggests Registered Dental Hygienist Meagan Lindquist Ramey.
